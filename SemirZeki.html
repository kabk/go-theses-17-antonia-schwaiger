Semir Zeki „all visual art is expressed through the brain and must therefore obey the laws of the brain whether in conception, execution or appreciation and no theory of aesthetics that is not substantially based on the activity of the brain is ever likely to be complete, let alone profound.“ Semir Zeki, Inner Visions: An Exploration of Art and the Brain, (New York: Oxford University Press, 1999). Across disciplines Zekis statement that art must obey the laws of the brain read as a statement of authority over art analysis from the privileged position of a neuroscientist. Taken in context these statement seemed to function more as a justification to conduct speculative research with in the field of neuroscience rather then an empirical assault on other disciplines In inner visions Zeki communicated that he was not trying to address the art experience comprehensively, and that he could not address emotions through the lens of neuroscience in art and also that he was trying to propose a formula for art but that it was his own curiosity that motivated him to explore the connections between art and the brain. At the same time, Zekis quote can be read, as an interdisciplinary provocation, suggesting that art follows the laws which are purview of neuroscience while at the same time dismissing the centuries long history of aethetics in the humanities. his Statement function as a protest call for those who were arguing for and against the value of neuroaesthetics and created a considerable amount of negative response. „ its people not their brains, that make and enjoy art“ Alva Noe Alva Noë, “Art and the Limits of Neuroscience,” New York Times: The Opinion Pages , (December 4, 2011), http://opinionator.blogs.nytimes.com/author/alva-noe (accessed June 29, 2012).
