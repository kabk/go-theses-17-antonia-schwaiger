Brooklyn Put A Bird On It

Posted by David

Looking back to history of aesthetic theory the first important contributions were provided by ancient Greek philosophers among them Plato, Aristoteles and Plotonius. During the eighteenth century, the term ‘aesthetic’ was employed to describe, among other things, the shape of an object, development of judgment, reflection as an attitude, description of an experience, and perception of value. Recently,,a new research field has been introduced by the neuroscientists Semir Zeki and Vilayanur S. Ramachandra. The field of neuroaesthtics. It developed out of fields of psychological aesthetics and neuroscience. It tries to describe the neurobiological principles of cognitive and affective processes which are created in a process of aesthetic experience and /or during creative activities. The field of neuroaesthtics has rapitly grown and diversified. For me neuroaesthetics is a new topic but, by researching the field, I became more and more intrigued by the exciting combination of art and science. With my thesis I want to give a short introduction into the field and show, even though neuroaesthetics is a field which is at the very beginning,, but is likely to have mayor impact on the modern definition of aesthetics.. The interplay of art and science will create new knowledge and understanding about neurobiology and aesthetic experience. Neuroaesthetics is a very controversial field and there are intense discussions about the need to link human experiences related to art with cognitive function oft he human brain.. I do not intend to deepen these discussions, but like to mention the most prominent argument against neuroaesthetics.: “Art does not need Neuroscience“ Which reflects the fact, that artists are not interested in an external „evaluation „ by neuroscientists.. In my opinion this concern comes from the habit that art advocates tend to enlist neuroscientists only as authoritative voices to reduce art and creativity to biologic processes. Based on my short encounter with neuroaesthetics, I am convinced that this field will broaden our understanding how artists express themselves and how art influences cognitive perception. I am sure such knowledge will generate new forms of art.
